Logic Decompiler Warnings

Decompiler warnings are generated when certain conditions are detected when analyzing the raw byte codes from a logic resource. Warnings do not prevent WinAGI from decompiling the logic, but indicate situations which you may want to address in the resulting code.

The warnings identified by WinAGI will be added inline to the decompiled source code at the position where they are found. In addition, the warnings are added to the Warnings and Errors List. You can double click on a warning to open the affected logic and be taken to the location of the warning.

None of the warnings identified during decompiling will prevent you from recompiling the logic. You can ignore them if you wish by just deleting the warning line from your source code.

Detailed explanations of each warning that may be encountered are provided below.

Where decompile warning messages include dynamic text, such as a resource type, or resource number, the dynamic text is represented by '<' and '>' characters, for example '<restype>'.

The <pos> argument points to the byte index position of the invalid value in the raw byte code. It is provided in case you wish to examine the data in a hex editor to further study the logic.
